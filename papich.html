Redesigning the experience of interaction between local and international students with a small country community within a university.

Observation & Interview

Participants (all the names are made up) :

    1. Ms. Kim - a graduate international student who has good background in Korean language, as she had 1 year of korean course before graduate school. Interviewed by Assem and observed by Aitolkyn. In her university she is the only representative from her country. Interestingly, at first she tried to initiate conversation with a young local student but failed. Eventually, she tried to talk to local university staff, and it was noticed that elder local was very friendly and interested in conversation. Notably, they started conversation by talking about their mutual friend. She pointed out that knowing local language did not guarantee to become closer friends with locals. Interesting points are that she could approach a local representative by buddy program where they met and mostly discussed K-pop, as a same interest topic.
    1. Robert - korean student, junior of physics department, male. Interviewed by Bauyrzhan. He was asked to have a conversation with an international freshman student. Robert was struggling to find a topic to talk about, however, had no struggles to keep up the conversation when his companion came up with a topic. After conversation, he shared his thoughts during it and he said that he was trying really hard to be cohesive and not to make any grammar/pronunciation mistakes.(for the moment of an interview, he was thought to be our target user, but in fact, he is not)
    1. Ms. Nam - international KAIST student, the only undergraduate from her country. Interviewed by Assem and Aitolkyn. What is unique about this participant is that she is a native English speaker and often feels awkwardness of locals when speaking to her, which motivates her to study Korean. It was noticed that she is an active participant of social student life, a member of several clubs and a lab, as she had to become more social after coming to Korea. An interesting experience of her interaction with locals occurred when local female students from her department approached her first and suggested to do HWs together. She thinks that since females are minority in their department, they tried to support her.
    1. Ms. Lee - international sophomore student, female. Ms. Lee - international sophomore student at KAIST having a comparatively small community in the university (around 10 students). Interviewed by Aitolkyn and observed by Assem. Ms. Lee was asked to initiate a conversation with a local student. Interesting observation was that even though they are from different departments and cultures, they could easily discuss on a specific topic related to the research of the local student. At the beginning the local student was not very responsive, probably because he was distracted from his work and did not talk too much. But it was noted that Ms. Lee constantly tried to express her interest and ask questions which led to more detailed discussions from him. Eventually the local student was engaged much greater and explained his project by using gestures and showing pictures.
    1. Ms. Park - international sophomore student, who befriended both locals and internationals, female. Our case of an extreme user. Interviewed by Miras. She was asked about her experience in befriending locals. Park mentioned that being somewhat knowledgeable in korean language played a big role in it, however, some of her buddies befriended her before she learned Korean. She also said that all of her local friends are from high school, as because of studies, she had no time to look for new acquaintances in the university itself.

  • Our extreme users: Ms. Kim and Ms. Nam, they are the only representatives of their country at a university.

  • How we recruited for the interview: at first we were targeting both locals and internationals seeking for communication, however, given the feedback of the studio session, we decided to narrow it down to internationals with small country communities within a university (15 or less representatives, to be precise).

Interesting moments noticed from the interview

  • Korean TV series appear to be an exceptionally good conversation topic.

  • More often than not, both sides lack confidence in their English speaking.

  • Elder generations are more willing to interact than younger generation.

  • Interactions in our university happen when they are in the same club or lab, but not outside of the stated environment.

  • Sharing the same problems enhances motivation to interact.

  • Most of the locals among those who have international friends, have been studying abroad prior to enrollment in the university.

Needs

  1. To have a support system (pieces of advice from fellow country seniors).
  2. To learn who is willing to open to other internationals and who prefers to stay within a closed community.
  3. To have occasions where people with the same interests can meet.
  4. To overcome nervous feelings in conversations.
  5. To have at least a general understanding of the local culture.
  6. To master local language.
  7. To socialize by joining student clubs.
  8. To schedule on the place and time of meetings.
  9. To organize gathering activities.
  10. Others to initiate conversations and acquaintances.

Insights

  1. Students from small country communities lack sources of advice like the one larger communities have by sharing experiences because they are restricted in terms of information.
  2. Target users fear to be annoying for locals with their attempts to communicate with them.
  3. Both locals and internationals lack confidence in their English language.
  4. Some locals are comfortable within their own community and lack motivation to communicate with foreigners.
  5. Our target users (from small country communities) tend to have more motivation to learn local language in comparison to internationals from larger country communities.
  6. Target users participate in social life more actively by joining social groups within a university.
  7. Some students are busy and lack time for social interactions.
  8. People have difficulties with meeting arrangements.
  9. Some locals tend to initiate conversations when feeling empathetically to internationals.
  10. Locals and internationals are unaware of each other’s cultural distinctive features.
  11. There are not enough events or occasions to meet people.

Studio feedback summary

What people liked:

  • The idea that we want to redesign this experience
  • Contents of “Interesting moments”
  • Clarity of the insights and clarity of our topic
  • Some found themselves amongst our target population

What people would like to improve:

  • Expand amount of time devoted for insights.
  • Our target users, they’d like us to narrow the range down and make it more specific.

How we addressed or will address the feedback:

  • We narrowed down our target to foreigners with a small country community(“small” means community consisting of less than 15 people)
  • We’ve changed the “Needs” a bit to fit the new target population.
  • We’ve interviewed additional people.
